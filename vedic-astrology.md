**Key differences from Western astrology:**

Vedic astrology uses the sidereal zodiac, which is based on the actual positions of constellations in the sky, while Western astrology uses the tropical zodiac aligned with the seasons. Due to the precession of the equinoxes, thereâ€™s currently about a 24-degree difference between them, meaning your sun sign in Vedic astrology is often different from your Western sign.

**Core components:**

It places significant emphasis on the Moon sign (Rashi) and the rising sign (Lagna), often considering them more important than the sun sign. The lunar mansions called Nakshatras divide the zodiac into 27 segments and are crucial for timing and personality analysis.

**Practical applications:**

Vedic astrology is traditionally used for predicting life events and timing through systems called Dashas (planetary periods), particularly the Vimshottari Dasha system.
